The NUJ Freelance Office has released the updated 2026 Freelance Rate Guide, reflecting current market conditions and recommended minimum rates for journalism across all platforms.

What's Changed

The updated guide includes revised rates for:

  • National newspapers: Print news stories, features, opinion columns
  • Digital outlets: Online-only publications, news websites
  • Broadcasting: Radio packages, television reports, podcast contributions
  • Photography: Editorial, events, stock usage rights
  • Sub-editing and production: Shift rates and per-piece rates

Why Rates Matter

Fair rates are essential for the sustainability of quality journalism. When publishers pay below the recommended minimum, they undermine the profession and make it harder for journalists to earn a living wage.

The NUJ encourages all freelance members to use the rate guide when negotiating fees and to report publishers who consistently offer below-minimum rates to the Freelance Office.
